About Suraah

In the 1930s, Anari Devi walked 300 km through the mountains of Uttarakhand, carrying provision for this 15 days of travel, solely to give her 5th-grade examination. I grew up hearing this story many times because it's the story of my grandmother. What breaks my heart is that even after a hundred year, the socio-economic conditions of the mountains haven't improved much.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

Providing a balanced and contextual education to the hill children to lead lives with their Head, Heart and Hands in harmony.

Vision

Uttarakhand with self-sustaining rural mountains, contributing locally and globally through their indigenous innovations.

History

In 1950-60s, my grandfather, a social reformer, served all his life in the hills, and led many regional movements in Uttarakhand. Later, my family settled in the Dehradun plains to seek economic stability. And the expectations from me has been that I live in a metro and pursue a stable career.
